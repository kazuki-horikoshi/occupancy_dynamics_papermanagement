\subsection{Comfort performance across different resolution of \ac{gcm}}

\SetPicSubDir{ch4-Results/agentic/Result_Agentic}
\label{subsec:ComfPerf}

Figure~\ref{fig:ComfAgentic}\subref{fig:ComfAchi} shows the comfort achievement ratio using \ac{sgcm}, \ac{dgcm} and {rt-gcm} for different subgroup sizes across four office rooms.
Figure~\ref{fig:ComfAgentic}\subref{fig:ComfImp} shows its improvement from the baseline control, that is assumed to be operated at \qty{24}{\celsius} through office hours.

The performance of \ac{rt-gcm} and \ac{dgcm} shows decreasing trend with similar ratio as subgroup size increases.
while the performance of \ac{sgcm} shows more rapid decrease as subgroup size increases.
In small size of subgroup such as 2--3, the comfort achievement ratio using \ac{rt-gcm} marks over \qtyrange{90}{95}{\percent}, while the control performance using \ac{dgcm} marks \qtyrange{80}{85}{\percent} with size of subgroup such as 2--5.

The improvement of comfort achievement ratio based on the \ac{sgcm} rapidly  shrinks and converges to zero in larger rooms, especially those with 5--6 users.
In contrast, the \ac{rtgcm} and \ac{dgcm} maintain a certain level of improvement from baseline even at the maximum room sizes considered, which range from 6 to 10 users.
Although both \ac{dgcm} and \ac{rtgcm} keeps certain improvement of performance from baselines, the gap between the \ac{dgcm} and \ac{rtgcm} increases as room size expands.
This suggests that, in larger rooms, the \ac{rtgcm} benefits from more dynamic occupant transitions, whereas the \ac{dgcm} cannot fully capture time-varying group comfort as occupancy changes.

\subsection{Effect to Control Adjustment}
\label{subsec:controleffect}
In previous subsection, the comfort performance of \ac{rt-gcm} is shown to keep higher by \qtyrange{15}{30}{percent} compared to \ac{sgcm} across room member sizes.
This subsection investigates the effect of \ac{rt-gcm} to control adjustment.
Since {sgcm} and {dgcm} assumes static occupant composition, the comtrol adjustment is only required in \ac{rt-gcm}-based control.

\autoref{fig:DynSpStepHist} shows the distribution of temperature adjustment magnitudes under the \ac{rt-gcm} across daily mean occupancy.
As daily occupancy increases, the range of temperature adjustment maginitude slowly shrinks.
However, the mean value keeps approximately around \qty{1.0}{\celsius}even at the highest occupancy levels.

Similarly \autoref{fig:DynSpStepHeatmap} shows the distribution of temperature adjustment magnitudes showed in distribution of subgroup-size and mean occupancy ratio at the control timestep.
The magnitude of this temperature range shrinks on both increase of sub-group size and mean occupancy ratio.
At small subgroup size such as $k=4$, the mean adjustment magnitude is over \qty{1.0}{\celsius} in most cases.
